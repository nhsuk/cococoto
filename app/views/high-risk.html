## NHS content on people at higher risk from coronavirus We have different types of content about people who are at a higher risk of getting seriously ill from coronavirus (COVID-19). ### People at high risk (clinically extremely vulnerable):
{% markdown %} People at high risk from coronavirus include people who: - have had an organ transplant - are having chemotherapy or antibody treatment for cancer, including immunotherapy - are having an intense course of radiotherapy (radical radiotherapy) for lung cancer - are having targeted cancer treatments that can affect the immune system (such as protein kinase inhibitors or PARP inhibitors) - have blood or bone marrow cancer (such as leukaemia, lymphoma or myeloma) - have had a bone marrow or stem cell transplant in the past 6 months, or are still taking immunosuppressant medicine - have been told by a doctor they you have a severe lung condition (such as cystic fibrosis, severe asthma or severe COPD) - have a condition that means they have a very high risk of getting infections (such as SCID or sickle cell) - are taking medicine that makes them much more likely to get infections (such as high doses of steroids or immunosuppressant medicine) - have a serious heart condition and are pregnant {% endmarkdown %}
This content is a summary of the main groups considered to be at high risk of becoming seriously ill from coronavirus – also known as clinically extremely vulnerable. People in this group are advised to stay at home at all times (called shielding). {% include('./includes/how-to-use.html') %} ### People at moderate risk (clinically vulnerable):
{% markdown %} People at moderate risk from coronavirus include people who: - are 70 or older - are pregnant - have a lung condition that's not severe (such as asthma, COPD, emphysema or bronchitis) - have heart disease (such as heart failure) - have diabetes - have chronic kidney disease - have liver disease (such as hepatitis) - have a condition affecting the brain or nerves (such as Parkinson's disease, motor neurone disease, multiple sclerosis or cerebral palsy) - have a condition that means they have a high risk of getting infections - are taking medicine that can affect the immune system (such as low doses of steroids) - are very obese (a BMI of 40 or above) {% endmarkdown %}
This content is a summary of the main groups considered to be at moderate risk of becoming seriously ill from coronavirus – also known as clinically vulnerable. People in this group are advised to take extra care to follow the advice on social distancing. {% include('./includes/how-to-use.html') %} ### Voice-friendly version:
{% markdown %} **Who is at higher risk from coronavirus?** Coronavirus can make anyone seriously ill. But some people are at higher risk. There are two levels of higher risk: moderate risk and high risk. People at moderate risk – also called clinically vulnerable – include anyone who is over 70 or pregnant. People at high risk – also called clinically extremely vulnerable – include people who’ve had an organ transplant or are having some types of cancer treatment. {% endmarkdown %}
This content is suited for voice assistants and chatbots. It provides a summary of the information in a voice-friendly structure – for example, by avoiding bullet points. {% include('./includes/how-to-use.html') %} ### Full version:
{% markdown %} See our full page on the NHS website about [who’s at higher risk from coronavirus](https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/coronavirus-covid-19/people-at-higher-risk-from-coronavirus/whos-at-higher-risk-from-coronavirus/). {% endmarkdown %}

The content has been adapted and simplified by our content team and checked by the clinical team at NHS Digital.
